Philosophy

How I think about
complex problems

01
Systems before tools

The architecture outlasts the technology. Before writing a line of code or choosing a platform, I map the system — data flows, failure modes, compliance boundaries. The right structure makes every tool choice reversible. The wrong one makes them all permanent.

02
Compliance is a
design spec

Regulatory requirements aren't constraints to work around — they're specifications to build to. Teams that treat compliance as an afterthought rebuild twice. I've worked under FINTRAC, OSFI, CRA, and PIPEDA long enough to know that building compliance-first saves months of rework and builds trust with the business.

03
AI should reduce toil,
not add it

The best AI integration disappears into the workflow. If your team has to relearn how they think to use the tool, the tool has failed. I measure success by how quietly the system operates — not how impressively it demos. Reducing back-office load by 35% means nothing caught on fire.
